1.
a. Claws are asymmetric, with one very large and one small (male). — Go to 2
b. Claws are small and symmetric (female). — Go to 13
2.
a. Eyestalks of crab are close together (a.k.a. "narrow-front"). — Go to 3
b. Eyestalks of crab are separated (a.k.a. "broad-front"). — Go to 7
3.
a. Major claw on male with large flattened fingers resembling shears, with almost no gape when closed. — Go to 4
b. Major claw on male with thinner, less flat fingers, not resembling shears, usually with clear gape when closed. — Go to 6
4.
a. Large fiddler with carapace breadth between 3 and 4cm. — male maracoani
b. Very large fiddler with carapace breadth > 4cm. — Go to 5
5.
a. Major pollex lacks conspicuous pits on outside surface. — male monilifera
b. Major pollex has conpspicuous pits no outside surface (can be hard to see in field). — male insignis or male ornata
6.
a. No long style on eye. — male hesperiae, male heteropleura, male intermedia, male major, male princeps, or male urvillei
b. Long style on eye on same side as major claw, with style about as long as eyestalk. — male stylifera
7.
a. Major claw on male is predominantly a single color. — Go to 8
b. Major claw on male is clearly multiple colors. — Go to 11
8.
a. Carapace is predominantly a solid single color. — Go to 9
b. Carapace is a mix of multiple colors (including blotches, stripes, or spots). — Go to 10
9.
a. Carapace is predominantly medium-to-dark blue; Limb joints have no distinctive red markings; Eyestalks appear gray-to-pale blue; Small fiddler with carapace breadth between 1 and 2cm. — male pugnax
b. Carapace is predominantly silvery-gray to gray-tan; Limb joints (including claws and walking legs) have distinctive reddish markings; Eyestalks appear white/gray, sometimes with a hint of orange; Medium fiddler with carapace breadth between 2 and 3cm. — male minax
10.
a. H-groove is silver-gray. — male pugilator
b. H-groove is rusty-red. — male panacea
11.
a. Major dactyl (movable finger) predominantly white; Major pollex is predominantly white; The outer manus (not including the pollex) of the major claw is predominantly pink; Tip of dactyl has a concave tooth with points at either end. — male inversa
b. Major dactyl (movable finger) pale pink-to-white, often with a darker red edge at the top; Major pollex is predominantly pale pink-to-white; The outer manus (not including the pollex) of the major claw is pink to red, often with darker red on the outer margins; Tip of dactyl comes to a normal point. — Go to 12
12.
a. Carapace is a mix of black and white blotches; Eyestalks appear white/gray; The outer manus (not including the pollex) of the major claw does not have large tubercles (bumps); Walking legs are predominantly a mix of black and white. — male occidentalis
b. Carapace is a mix of blue and black blotches (usually more blue than black, but not always); Eyestalks appear green; The outer manus (not including the pollex) of the major claw is covered with large tubercles (bumps); Walking legs are predominantly red. — male chlorophthalmus
13.
a. Eyestalks of crab are close together (a.k.a. "narrow-front"). — female hesperiae, female heteropleura, female insignis, female intermedia, female major, female maracoani, female monilifera, female ornata, female princeps, female stylifera, or female urvillei
b. Eyestalks of crab are separated (a.k.a. "broad-front"). — female chlorophthalmus, female inversa, female minax, female occidentalis, female panacea, female pugilator, or female pugnax
